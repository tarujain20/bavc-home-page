Property Found for Bay Area Vipassana Center

After evaluating more than 40 properties over the last 4 years, the Bay Area Vipassana Trust is happy to announce that we are now in escrow to purchase a property for a Vipassana Meditation Center in the Bay Area. The development of a Center in this conveniently accessible, peaceful location would enable more people from the Bay Area and beyond to develop in Dhamma by providing more opportunities to sit and serve.

The ability to purchase this ideal property for a future fully operating center is contingent upon receiving sufficient donations from old students to meet the purchase price by March 30. We currently have pledges for $400k and we will need donations of the full purchase price of $1.98 million dollars by March 30th in order to determine if we can proceed with the purchase of the property. If the funds do not materialize in time, the trust will have to let the property go.

Centrally located in California 
close to San Jose

The 54 acres property is located off of Redwood Retreat Road in a rural area in Gilroy, the property is 35 miles from San Jose airport, 65 miles from San Francisco or Oakland airport and 30 miles from Santa Cruz.

PHASE 1
The property is valued at $1.98M by the seller. Additionally, we would need about $6M for Phase 1 construction.

If we are able to purchase this property the plan is to start development for 60 to 80 students sometime in early 2019.

There is a large barn on the property, which could be converted into a multi-purpose building for use while the rest of the center is built up over time.

PHASE 2
The goal would be to expand the capacity of the center to
accommodate a total of 150 students, servers, and teachers,
which would be done in future development phases as
donations become available.

The Buddha declared, "The gift of Dhamma is the greatest gift."

All three California centers consistently have long waitlists, and the interest seems to only be increasing. A center so close to this huge urban hub will attract many more people to Vipassana courses, both in and outside of the Bay Area, and it will serve the diverse community of old students dwelling in the region. By contributing to the purchase of this property, old students can personally share in providing the gift of Dhamma to many more people.
